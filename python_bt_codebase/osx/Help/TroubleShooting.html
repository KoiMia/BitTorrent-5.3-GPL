Troubleshooting

Errors are displayed in the log, choose View > Log Drawer.

Nothing happens.

  • BitTorrent will not do anything until you start a download by opening a torrent file. Torrent files end with ".torrent" and are obtained through web sites, e-mail, instant message, etc... There is no search built into BitTorrent.

Not connected to any peers.

  • Tracker may be down, click on the torrent and select View > Log Drawer to see if there is an error.
  • Torrent may be "dead" and there are no peers left to connect to.

Downloaded part of the file but then downloading stops. Uploading may continue.

  • The torrent is "stuck." Parts of the file are not to be found on any peers. The torrent will remain stuck until a seed or peer shows up with the missing parts

The download is progressing very slowly.

  • Fresh torrents with many peers usually give better performance.
  • Torrents with many seeds give better performance.
  • If you are behind a firewall or AirPort base station, make sure the port used by BitTorrent is forwarded to your computer. While not necessary, it ensures you get the most performance out of every torrent and is highly reccommended.
